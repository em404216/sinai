Kharzet El-Shaq ‚Äî A Hidden Gem in the Heart of Shaq Tiniya Valley

Kharzet El-Shaq lies at the upper reaches of Shaq Tiniya Valley. It is a natural rock-carved water pool, crowned by a small seasonal waterfall formed by rainfall ‚Äî one that often flows throughout the year. The surrounding landscape is a dramatic mix of stone formations and serene wilderness.

The area around the pool is rich with ancient mountain gardens, built by the ancestors of the Jebeliya tribe, who cultivated the land around reliable water sources ‚Äî a living testament to their deep connection with the land.

Geographically, the site is framed by two prominent mountains:

  • To the north: Jabal Naagah (Mount Naagah)
  • To the south: Jabal Abbas Pasha

El Galt Al Azraq is the largest permanent water pool in the highlands of Saint Catherine. It lies beneath Wadi Al-Tabaq, northeast of Jabal Bab El Dunya, and is considered one of the most important natural stops along hiking trails in the area.

The site offers a perfect rest point for hikers, where they can swim in its cool waters and relax under the shade of surrounding willow trees ‚Äî all amidst a rare mountain setting that blends serenity, isolation, and raw natural beauty.

Wadi El-Kid is one of the most stunning valleys in South Sinai. It begins with a wide entrance and follows an easy, winding path with few obstacles, making it ideal for hiking and gentle exploration. At the upper end of the valley, to the northwest, lies the Red Valley, a narrow gorge between steep cliffs where rare desert plants grow ‚Äî species found nowhere else in the region. This makes it a unique ecological gem and a must-visit destination for lovers of pristine nature.
