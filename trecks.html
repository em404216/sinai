Long treks in Sinai range from 3 to 10 days and are divided into three main routes — which can also be combined for a more immersive journey.

Main Trekking Routes

Volcanic Peaks Route

This route covers the central highlands of Sinai, home to Egypt’s tallest and most iconic mountains. It follows a network of trails connecting the majestic peaks around Mount Catherine via elevated valleys between the mountains.

Key mountains include: Mount Catherine, Mount Mousa (Mount Sinai), Mount Abbas Basha, Mount Abu Qron, Mount Abu 'Alda, Mount Bab El Dunya, Mount Naagah, Mount El Banat, Mount El Hashwa, and the Red Mountain.

Western Mountain Route

This route begins in Saint Catherine and leads westward across the highlands toward the coast of the Gulf of Suez. Along the way, it passes through remote valleys and the ancient monasteries of Ramhan and Antoush, culminating with the ascent of Mount Umm Shomer — the second-highest peak in Sinai, offering breathtaking views of the western coastline.

Valley Routes

These treks connect the intricate network of valleys surrounding Saint Catherine with other famous desert valleys nestled between the mountains.

Notable valleys include: Wadi El Talaa, Wadi Itlah, Wadi El Freya, Wadi Ghurba, Wadi Aslaf, Wadi El Tarfa, Wadi El Nesb, Wadi Esla, and Wadi El Zawatein.

Canyon & Sandstone Mountain Route

This route explores the region's iconic sandstone formations and deep canyons, offering a unique desert experience.

Highlights include: Jebel El Makhroom, Jebel Malihis, Jebel El Barqa, Jebel El Matamir, the Colored Canyon, the White Canyon, Arada Canyon, and the remote oasis of Ain Hudra.

Day Trips

St. Catherine’s Monastery and Mount Sinai Tour

This tour offers two options:

  • Morning Trip: Visit St. Catherine’s Monastery and then hike up Mount Sinai to witness a breathtaking sunset from the summit.
  • Night Trip: Begin the hike at night to reach the summit in time for a stunning sunrise, followed by a visit to the monastery in the morning.

Valley Walks Near St. Catherine

These are easy, relaxed walks through one of the nearby valleys. You'll enjoy a gentle hike, have lunch in nature, and return by the end of the day.

Mountain Climbing Trips

These hikes involve ascending one of Sinai’s iconic peaks, such as Mount Catherine, Mount Abbas Basha, or the Red Mountain — perfect for those seeking adventure and panoramic views.

Desert Safari Tours

In the eastern part of Sinai lies a striking desert landscape filled with sandy mountains and surreal formations. These tours may include:

  • Jebel el-Makhroom
  • Jebel el-Matamir
  • The Nawamis Site (prehistoric tombs)
  • Ain Hudra Oasis
  • Exploring surrounding canyons such as the White Canyon, Arada Canyon, and the Rainbow Canyon.

🧭 What to Pack for Your Sinai Safari

Be prepared for an unforgettable journey into the heart of Sinai’s wild beauty. Here's your essential checklist:

🥾 Clothing & Gear

  • Comfortable hiking shoes (non-slip)
  • Light, breathable clothes
  • Light jacket for evenings
  • Hat or headscarf for sun
  • Sunglasses for desert glare

🧴 Personal Care

  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Wet wipes or sanitizer

💧 Food & Hydration

  • Reusable water bottle
  • Light snacks (nuts, dried fruit)

🔦 Essentials

  • Headlamp or flashlight + batteries
  • Daypack for personal items

📱 Safety & Contact

  • Fully charged phone + power bank
  • Any personal medication
  • Copy of ID or passport
